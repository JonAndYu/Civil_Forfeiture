Introduction

Forfeiture refers to the legal process where someone loses their property or assets as a result of their involvement in illegal activities. In some types of forfeiture, such as civil forfeiture, law enforcement may seize property without any evidence of criminal activity which leaves the burden on the owner to legally fight for the return of the property. Unfortunately, this burden disproportionately victimizes lower income individuals who may not have the necessary financial backing to reclaim their property.

The goal of our visualization is to allow concerned US citizens to uncover potential inequitable forfeiture trends that occurred throughout the country. The original dataset contained many incomplete observations so imputation was utilized to fill in unknown property and conviction types while still maintaing the pre-existing national and state ratios.
